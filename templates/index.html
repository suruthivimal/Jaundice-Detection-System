📸 Image Guidelines

For the highest classification accuracy, please ensure your photo follows these protocols:

  • Natural Lighting: Take photos in daylight. Avoid yellow indoor bulbs or direct smartphone flash.
  • Focus & Position: Keep the forehead centered and capture it from straight-on, not at an angle.
  • No Obstructions: Ensure there are no hairs, hats, or plasters covering the infant's forehead.
  • Crop Area: Crop the image to show only the forehead skin, avoiding eyes or eyebrows.

💡 Bilirubin & Jaundice

Neonatal jaundice occurs when a baby has high levels of bilirubin, a yellow pigment produced during normal breakdown of red blood cells.

If bilirubin levels remain elevated and untreated, it can lead to complications. Bilirubin dermally manifests as yellowing of skin and eyes.

JaundiceCare acts as a preliminary screening mechanism to flag infants that require prompt clinical pediatric consultation.
